# Map Your Home Course for Plans2Putts

Thanks for helping out! This takes about 20–30 minutes and all you need is a
laptop with a browser. No app to install, no account to create, no glasses needed.

## What you're doing

You're placing pins on a satellite photo of your course — marking tee boxes,
greens, hazards, doglegs, and layup spots. This data powers a golf rangefinder
app for Meta's smart glasses that shows live yardage on a heads-up display.

## Before you start

Pull up your course's scorecard or hole-by-hole map (most courses post these on
their website). Use it as a reference so you know exactly what to mark on each hole.

## Step by step

1. Open this link on your laptop:
   **https://harriskevint-hue.github.io/plans2putts/course-mapper.html**

2. Type your course name in the "Course name" field at the top right.

3. Zoom and pan the satellite map to find your course. Zoom in close — you'll
   be able to see tee boxes, greens, bunkers, and water clearly from above.

4. For each hole (use the Hole dropdown to switch between 1–18):

   - Select **Tee** and click on the tee box
   - Select **Front** and click on the front edge of the green
   - Select **Center** and click the middle of the green
   - Select **Back** and click the back edge of the green
   - Select **Hazard**, type a label (like "right fairway bunker"), and click it
   - Select **Dogleg** if the fairway curves — click the corner point
   - Select **Layup** if there's a key layup spot — type a label (like "short of water"), click it
   - Set the **Par** for the hole

   You can drag any pin to fine-tune its position.

5. **Export often!** Click "Export JSON" to save your file. If you close the
   page without exporting, your work is lost. To resume later, open the mapper
   and click "Import JSON" to reload your saved file.

6. When all 18 holes are done, send the exported JSON file to Kevin.

## Tips

- **Zoom in close** — the more zoomed in you are, the more precisely you can
  place pins. You can see individual bunkers and green contours.
- **Use your scorecard as a guide** — it tells you exactly what hazards exist
  on each hole so you don't miss anything.
- **You know your course** — if there's a ditch, OB line, or trouble spot that
  isn't on the scorecard or visible from the air, add it as a hazard. Your
  local knowledge is what makes this data better than commercial databases.
- **Don't worry about flag position** — pin locations change daily. Just mark
  the front, center, and back of the green.
- **Doglegs** — mark the corner where the fairway turns. Golfers want to know
  "how far to the corner" to pick the right club.
- **Layups** — mark where you'd want to lay up short of trouble (e.g., "150 to
  the water, lay up here"). Type a label so it's clear what you're staying
  short of.

## What to map (quick reference)

| Feature | Tool | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tee box | Tee (blue) | One per hole |
| Front of green | Front (light green) | Front edge |
| Center of green | Center (green) | Middle of the putting surface |
| Back of green | Back (dark green) | Back edge |
| Bunkers | Hazard (orange) | Label each one (e.g. "greenside left") |
| Water | Hazard (orange) | Label it (e.g. "pond right of fairway") |
| OB / trouble | Hazard (orange) | Label it (e.g. "OB left") |
| Fairway turn | Dogleg (yellow) | Where the fairway bends |
| Layup spot | Layup (purple) | Where to land short of trouble |
