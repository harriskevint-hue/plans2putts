# White Horse Golf Club — Mapping Reference

Use this alongside the course mapper. For each hole, it tells you what to place
and where to look on the satellite view. Open the hole's YouTube video if you
need a better angle on anything.

Course mapper: https://harriskevint-hue.github.io/plans2putts/course-mapper.html

---

## Hole 1 — Par 4
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, diagonal cross bunker at the landing area
**Notes:** Relatively open. The cross bunker sits diagonally across the entrance
to the landing zone. Room left for safe play. No dogleg.

## Hole 2 — Par 4
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, bunkers right of green, deep hollows past landing area
**Notes:** Ideal drive is right of center. Deep hollows past the landing area on
both sides — mark as hazards. Green slopes left to right. Steep drop-off right
of green.

## Hole 3 — Par 5
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, DOGLEG (sweeping right-to-left), fairway bunker
dissecting the fairway (layup reference)
**Notes:** Genuine three-shot hole. Fairway slopes right-to-left on the dogleg.
Mark the dogleg corner. The bunker dissecting the fairway is a key layup
landmark — mark it as a hazard AND consider a layup point just short of it.
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/embed/yz692L3N2Mo

## Hole 4 — Par 3
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, two deep greenside bunkers, lay-up area left of green
**Notes:** Dramatic par 3 with alternate routes. Two deep bunkers punish
under-clubbing. Ample fairway left for a lay-up — mark a LAYUP point there.
Conservative route is left.

## Hole 5 — Par 4
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, bunkers right of fairway, HIDDEN bunker left side
**Notes:** Premium on accuracy. Important — large bunker hidden from view guards
the LEFT side of fairway and green. This is exactly the kind of thing the
satellite might not make obvious. Look carefully. Trouble all around the green.

## Hole 6 — Par 3
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, deep bunkers right of green
**Notes:** Large green but difficult target. Deep bunkers on the right are the
main trouble.

## Hole 7 — Par 4
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, greenside bunkers, valley (hazard)
**Notes:** Tee shot over an 80-yard valley — dramatic drop. Mark the valley as a
hazard. Second shot is downhill (may play a club shorter). Small well-bunkered
green. Best drive is right of center.
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/embed/aHWjuX8OuwA

## Hole 8 — Par 5 (568 yards)
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, drive bunkers, second-shot bunkers, two deep bunkers
30 yards short of green (LAYUP point)
**Notes:** Very long. Bunkers on both the drive and second shot. Two deep bunkers
thirty yards short of green — mark a LAYUP point short of these for players not
going for it in two.
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/embed/0shmueCYEWg

## Hole 9 — Par 4
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, fairway bunkers
**Notes:** Straight-away. Best drive just left of center. Difficult approach shot
to the green — possibly the hardest on the course.

## Hole 10 — Par 4
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, DOGLEG LEFT, deep swale on the inside of the dogleg
**Notes:** Elevated dogleg left. Mark the dogleg corner at the top of the ridge.
Players cutting the corner find a deep swale — mark it as a hazard.
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/embed/n14nv3LO2xk

## Hole 11 — Par 4
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, massive waste bunker along entire right side
**Notes:** Spectacular view. Waste bunker runs the full right side of the hole —
mark it as a long hazard. Shouldn't be in play unless drive goes far right.
Good drive left of center leaves a short iron in.

## Hole 12 — Par 4
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, greenside bunkers, side slope (hazard right)
**Notes:** Short but dramatic, plays up a deep valley. Use the left side slope —
ball feeds back to the valley. Missed tee shot right leaves a semi-blind
approach. Elevated, well-bunkered green.

## Hole 13 — Par 3
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, bunkers right, large mound right, steep slope behind
green
**Notes:** Picturesque (Mt. Baker view). Several bunkers and a mound protect the
right side. If the flag is back, the ball can trickle off the green down a steep
slope — mark that back slope as a hazard.

## Hole 14 — Par 5
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, narrow second-shot landing area, lateral hazard behind
and right of green, LAYUP point, drop-off right into woods
**Notes:** Doesn't show its teeth until the second shot. Narrow landing area with
severe drop-off right into woods. Lateral hazard behind AND right of the green —
critical for players going in two. Mark a LAYUP point for the safe second shot.
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/embed/c9L91aoBavc

## Hole 15 — Par 4
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, fairway bunkers short of green, large hill hiding
green
**Notes:** Dramatic from elevated tees. Large hill hides the green — mark the hill
area. Fairway bunkers short of the green are the main trouble. Landing area is
generous.

## Hole 16 — Par 5 (longest par 5)
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, deep fairway bunkers left, trees right, LAYUP point
at second landing area
**Notes:** Longest par 5 — must be long AND accurate. Deep bunkers and rough
left, trees right. Best strategy: lay up with a long iron to the large second
landing area — mark that as a LAYUP point. Long, well-protected green.
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/embed/HJcm2rAZkYg

## Hole 17 — Par 3 (second shortest hole)
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, deep bunkers around green, safe area short-right
**Notes:** Short but extremely challenging. Green looks like a bowl with deep
bunkers interspersed. Room short-right for a bail-out — mark as a LAYUP.

## Hole 18 — Par 4
**Mark:** Tee, green F/C/B, bunkers flanking fairway, TWO ACRE LAKE, LAYUP point
short-right of lake
**Notes:** Beautiful finishing hole. Elevated tees. Two-acre lake is the dominant
hazard — mark it. From the landing area: decision to carry the water to the
green or lay up short-right. Mark a LAYUP point for the safe play short of the
water. Long, undulating green.
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/embed/M93iVDfzfmU
